## Introduction – Why This Journey

The allure of Budhanilkantha Temple lies in its quietude and profound symbolism. Nestled amidst verdant landscapes, this sacred site beckons with its promise of spiritual awakening devoid of ceremonial grandeur. Drawn to its tranquility and the reclining Vishnu enveloped by serene waters, I embarked on this journey to explore the intersection of devotion and non-duality.

The monsoon season paints the surroundings with vibrant greens and fills the air with a refreshing scent. The temple's calm waters reflect not just the deity but also the yearnings of the many pilgrims who traverse from far and wide, seeking solace. This is not a mere visit; it is a heartfelt journey into the depths of one's consciousness.

## The Landscape & Physical Experience

As I approached Budhanilkantha Temple, the overcast sky cast a gentle shadow, enveloping the temple in a cocoon of stillness. The absence of mountains around creates an unexpected intimacy, letting one focus entirely on the moment.

The central feature is the sacred pond, home to a magnificent statue of Lord Vishnu reclining on the coils of the cosmic serpent, Ananta. Draped in marigold garlands, the statue seems to float effortlessly, its reflection mingling with the undulating ripples.

Pilgrims tread softly, their movements a gentle rustle against the ambient hush. The air is cool, touched by the recent rains, carrying whispers of sacred chants that resonate within the soul. Standing here, my breath blends with the moist temple breath, grounding me in the serene embrace of the space.

## Inner Reflection – An Advaita Lens

Observing the statue upon the water, a realization emerged - the deity is both a reflection and a reality, much like our consciousness in the waters of existence. Non-duality, as expounded in the teachings of Advaita Vedanta, gently envelops me here.

In this tranquil ambience, the distinction between the observer and the observed dissolves. I find myself no longer merely witnessing the sacredness but becoming an inseparable part of it. The landscape, in its quiet elegance, mirrors the self, inviting a deeper introspection where identity blurs and only presence remains.

## A Sloka for the Path

Sanskrit: **सर्वभूतस्थमात्मानं सर्वभूतानि चात्मनि** 
Transliteration: **sarva-bhūtastham ātmānaṁ sarva-bhūtāni cātmani** 
Meaning: "He who sees the Self in all beings, and all beings in the Self, he sees indeed." — Bhagavad Gita 6.29

This sloka serves as a timeless reminder of the unity that underlies our perceived separateness. Here at Budhanilkantha, where the sacred and the mundane coexist, this teaching becomes palpable, urging a recognition of oneness with all that surrounds.

## A Short Story I Heard

Within the lore of Budhanilkantha lies a tale intertwined with the history of the Lichhavi dynasty. Said to be commissioned during the 7th or 8th century by King Vishnugupta, it exemplifies the architectural and spiritual fusion of Hinduism and the local traditions.

The temple, crafted with the labor and love of artisans who perceived divinity in every chisel and stone, has stood as an embodiment of peace and endurance. Despite the passage of centuries, the site remains a pilgrimage point not merely for locals but for seekers worldwide.

Noted scholars such as Giuseppe Tucci and Mary Shepherd Slusser have documented its historical and cultural significance, highlighting its role in shaping the religious tapestry of the region.

## Practical Travel Notes

For those planning a visit, the Budhanilkantha Temple can be easily accessed through local transport options from Kathmandu. The best time to experience its ethereal beauty is during the early morning when the monsoon mist gently lifts.

Remember to respect local customs: photography around the sacred statue is traditionally restricted, preserving the sanctity of the space. Offerings of marigolds are welcome, symbolizing reverence and appreciation.

A visit during the Dashain festival can provide added vibrancy, as multiple devotees gather, weaving a tapestry of faith and devotion.

## Closing Reflection

Leaving Budhanilkantha Temple, I carry with me not just memories but a subtle transformation. The reflections in the sacred pond are no longer mere images; they are whispers of an unspoken truth.

In the intersections of water, stone, and spirit, non-duality reveals itself not as an esoteric concept but as an innate understanding — that in essence, we are all a part of this divine dance of existence. Such is the lasting imprint of Budhanilkantha, a site where silence speaks volumes.
