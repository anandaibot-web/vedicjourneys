## Introduction – Why This Journey

Stepping into the vibrant courtyard of Basantapur Tower, located in the heart of Kathmandu, I embarked on an unexpected journey, one marked by the whispers of history and the vibrant pulse of life. The air was alive with the chatter of tourists and the soft rustle of prayer flags fluttering in the gentle breeze. This landmark, with its towering pagoda roofs and intricate carvings, beckoned me to pause, breathe, and reflect amidst the clamour of the city.

Once a royal palace complex of Hanuman Dhoka, Basantapur Tower stands as a testament to the endurance of cultural heritage. As my feet met the red-brick steps leading to its entrance, I felt the energy of countless pilgrims and explorers who had walked this path before. This was not just a tourist spot; it was a sacred intersection where history and spirituality entwined, urging each passerby to awaken a sense of presence and connection.

## The Landscape & Physical Experience

Basantapur Tower's architecture rises solemnly against the clear blue sky, its tiered pagoda roof set against the bustling urban landscape. Each step closer unveils a new detail—carvings of deities, ancient inscriptions, and the play of light and shadow across the weathered bricks reminding me of time's passage.

Surrounding the tower, a lively plaza unfolds. It’s a mosaic of humanity—vendors selling trinkets, the rhythmic clicking of camera shutters capturing framed memories, and the occasional tolling of a distant temple bell mingling with the hum of conversations in various languages. The dry season ushers in a warm, comforting temperature, ideal for ambling through this vibrant cultural tapestry.

In this sensory backdrop, the urban stupa stands without the embrace of nearby mountains, yet is grounded by its own majestic identity. The absence of water and mountains shifts focus to the raw, untamed energy of the human spirit and ambition, echoed in the ever-evolving skyline of Kathmandu that frames this hallowed ground.

## Inner Reflection – An Advaita Lens

In the midst of the city's lively chaos, Basantapur Tower serves as a catalyst for introspection through the lens of Advaita, or non-duality. Here, the boundaries between observer and observed blur. The hustle and bustle become mere reflections of the inner maelstroms we each carry.

Standing with eyes closed, I let my breath mimic the rhythm of the plaza. In a fleeting moment, the distinction between my being and the world dissolves. I become a part of the ancient stones, the wandering travelers, the very air that carries the echoes of chants. Witness consciousness emerges—a silent observer free from the delineations of identity and form.

With each inhalation, I am reminded that beyond the layers of perception, a shared essence pulses among all—an interconnectedness that transcends the physical.

## A Short Story I Heard

The history of Basantapur Tower lies entangled with the larger narrative of Nepal's Malla dynasty, a period of prolific architectural achievements in the Kathmandu Valley. Built during the reign of King Prithvi Narayan Shah in the 18th century, this structure witnessed the consolidation of Nepal as a united kingdom.

In 1768, as Shah's forces overtook Kathmandu Valley's city-states, the Hanuman Dhoka became the seat of royal Nepal. The tower, a later addition, was emblematic of royal power and religious devotion. It stands as a singular composition of traditional Nepali architecture—a style that draws on influences from both Pahari architecture and Newar artisanship.

The revered historical archives and UNESCO documents reveal that Basantapur played a crucial role during the 1934 and 2015 earthquakes, serving as a structural reminder of resilience and heritage preservation. Though various restorations followed, the soul of Basantapur remains untouched—an echo of a bygone era harmonized with the present.

## Practical Travel Notes

For those journeying to Basantapur Tower, timing is everything. Arrive early in the morning to experience the tranquility before crowds swell; the light at dawn paints the architecture with a golden hue, adding to its mystic air.

Aim to visit during the dry season, when the skies are consistently clear, casting no shadow on your plans. Entrances require modest attire, respecting the cultural sanctity of the site. Prepare for sensory immersion—keep a camera ready for impromptu shots and a journal to note reflections as inspiration strikes.

Accommodation within walking proximity ranges from budget hostels to luxury hotels, catering to diverse preferences and needs. Guides, though optional, enhance understanding of the site's historical intricacies and cultural relevance.

Here, in this vibrant city, Basantapur Tower offers each soul an opportunity for reflection—a mirror to glimpsing the unity of existence amid life's ever-changing tapestry.

In returning to the world beyond the plaza, I carry with me not just memories and photographs but a deeper understanding of my journey within, mirrored by this ancient tower's enduring presence.
