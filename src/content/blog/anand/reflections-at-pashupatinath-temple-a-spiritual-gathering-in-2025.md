## Introduction – Why This Journey

Standing on the grounds of Pashupatinath Temple on June 25, 2025, I found myself surrounded by a group of fellow yatris, each of us drawn to this sacred place by a common yearning. Pashupatinath is not merely an architectural wonder but a pulsating heart of spiritual confluence, where culture and devotion intermingle seamlessly. Nestled within the vibrancy of Kathmandu, this temple radiates an aura that beckons to those seeking deeper understanding. As a traveler, I've always sought destinations that challenge not just my physical boundaries, but also the inner landscapes of my being. This journey was driven by a desire to connect with the profound energy that Pashupatinath epitomizes. The temple, with its intricate brickwork and golden rooftops shimmering under the clear blue sky, offered more than a visual feast; it promised an opportunity for introspection and connection, inviting us to explore the ancient wisdom residing within ourselves.

## The Landscape & Physical Experience

Arriving at the temple complex, I was immediately struck by the harmonious blend of architecture and nature. The red brickwork contrasted beautifully against the verdant backdrop of trees, their leaves rustling gently under a warm, inviting sun. As I walked, the texture of the stone pathways underfoot was a grounding reminder of the countless pilgrims who had traversed these paths before me. The temple's shikhara soared skyward, its golden rooftops dazzling in the sunlight, a beacon of divine presence amidst the earthly realm. Despite the absence of water, the air was thick with the fragrance of incense mingled with the earthy scent of dust and stone. Nearby, the chatter of birds provided a melodic counterpoint to the hum of activities from devotees gathered around. The high-pitched chimes of temple bells resonated through the air, punctuating moments with their clarity. Engulfed in this sacred atmosphere, the physical sensations—warmth on my skin, the weight of my backpack, the rhythmic sounds—each contributed to the deepened awareness of being fully present, not only in this revered space but also within myself.

## Inner Reflection – An Advaita Lens

The experience at Pashupatinath Temple offered a unique occasion to delve into the philosophy of Advaita, which emphasizes non-duality and the dissolution of ego. As I witnessed the collective energy of devotees, each person engaged in their own prayerful ritual yet unified in purpose, a sense of interconnectedness unfolded. Here, the lines between the individual and the universal blurred. The ego, often a barrier between 'I' and 'the world,' began to dissipate in the presence of such a gathering. It was as if the vibrant chaos of human activity held a mirror to the inner landscape of mind and spirit, encouraging the witness consciousness to emerge. In this space, I observed my own thoughts as clouds drifting across the sky, unattached and transient. This subtle realization, drawn from physically being amidst the people and ancient structures, illuminated the path towards embracing oneness, a core principle of Advaita Vedanta.

## A Sloka for the Path

"अहमात्मा गुदाकेश सर्वभूताशयस्थित:। अहम् आदिश्च मध्यं च भूतानामन्त एव च॥" (Bhagavad Gita 10.20)

**Transliteration:** Ahamātmā gudākeśa sarvabhūtāśayasthitaḥ; aham ādiś ca madhyaṁ ca bhūtānām anta eva ca.

**English Meaning:** I am the Self, O Gudakesha, seated in the hearts of all creatures. I am the beginning, the middle, and the end of all beings.

**Reference:** Bhagavad Gita 10.20

In reflecting upon this verse during the visit to Pashupatinath, the ancient sentiments became palpable. Standing amidst the crowd, each individual a vessel of myriad experiences, this sloka offered a reminder of the interconnected nature of existence. The temple, a symbol of beginning and end, with its red brick walls resonating with history, mirrored the cycle of life and consciousness. Here, the teaching of being the Self in every heart took root, resonating with the collective energy of the pilgrims. Each devotee, engaging in personal reflection, embodied the concept of unity, serving as living manifestations of the eternal truth encapsulated in this scripture.

## Practical Travel Notes

Arriving at Pashupatinath Temple was a sensory experience in itself. As we entered the main gates, the vibrant colors of traditional clothing worn by fellow travelers and the intricate designs on the temple structure were the first things to catch the eye. One particular highlight was the hanging flower garlands that added bursts of color against the earthy backdrop. Although the air was warm, a pleasant breeze tempered the heat, making the exploration comfortable. Walking along the stone paths, I noticed a tactile change when stepping inside, as coolness emanated from the shaded interiors of the temple corridors. The place was alive with sounds—soft murmurs of prayers whispered by devotees, punctuated by the ringing of bells that echoed across the complex. The scent of incense wafted through the air, mixing with the aroma of food from nearby stalls, grounding the spiritual with the earthly. Moving with the group, I was keenly aware of the camaraderie among us, a shared purpose that transcended language and background, binding us together in this beautiful journey.

## Closing Reflection

As the day drew to a close and the golden light of dusk began to drape the complex, I took a moment to sit quietly, reflecting on the day's emotions and insights. The sun setting behind the temple cast long shadows, stretching the memories of our journey into the realm of timelessness. Pashupatinath Temple had not only been a destination but a threshold, inviting introspection and awakening. Reflecting on this experience, I realized that the journey had not led outward to a distant horizon but inward, into the quiet recesses of understanding. It was here, amidst the bustling devotion, the solemn rituals, and the ancient stones, that the layers of complexity in spiritual pursuit revealed their simple truth: our search for meaning is not in isolation but in seeing oneself within the vast tapestry of life. In this shared experience, clarity emerged, a silent echo of belonging to something far greater than individual identity—a unity that was both humbling and enriching.
