## Introduction – Why This Journey

The journey from Darchen to Dalmala Pass is not just a physical traverse across the rugged expanse of Mount Kailash but an introspective pilgrimage, a rite of passage that calls for endurance and reflection. This path, often regarded as the most arduous day of the pilgrimage, is imbued with an intense charm that few other spiritual odysseys can match. It's 2025, and I find myself drawn to this ancient route, marinating in its history and spiritual significance. There's something deeply compelling about walking this path—a blend of myth and reality. The crisp chill of the morning air bites at my cheeks as I begin my ascent, the thick wool of my gloves offering little solace against the persistent cold. Yet, it’s this very discomfort that grounds me in the present moment, every footfall a reminder of my connection to the earth beneath.

## The Landscape & Physical Experience

The terrain from Darchen to the Dalmala Pass is a canvas of raw, untouched beauty where each element conspires to create a scene of majestic desolation. Jagged peaks rise sharply against the backdrop of a partly cloudy sky, crowned with whispering wisps of clouds that seem to conceal and reveal secrets in quick succession. As our party of pilgrims winds its way up the rocky path, the distinct clattering of hooves against stone rises amidst the silence—the sound curiously comforting in this vast emptiness. The path is an arduous one, each step heavy under the weight of altitude and thin air, leaving my thighs burning with an appreciative ache.

Our horses tread cautiously, their hooves clattering against the cold, unyielding rocks, their breath visible in the chilling air. I feel the tension in the shared labor of reaching this spiritual summit; it links us together—a woven tapestry of shared human experience against an indifferent wilderness. The sun, when it emerges, slants and plays across the path, creating stark contrasts of shadow and light that dance upon the stones. Despite the harshness, there is an undeniable vibrancy in the scrubs and the occasional flecks of lichen clinging stubbornly to life. High-altitude vegetation speckles the barren landscape, painting the earth with hues of sage and muted yellows. This kaleidoscope of natural elements instills within me a profound respect for the resilience of nature and a reflection of my own journey.

## Inner Reflection – An Advaita Lens

As I gaze upon the colossal expanse of nature, marked by the jagged peaks partially obscured by clouds, the principles of Advaita Vedanta echo softly in my consciousness. Standing amidst such grandeur, the illusion of separation dissolves into a recognition of oneness. The path, the mountains, the people—all form part of an interconnected tapestry of existence. Advaita teaches us about non-duality, the ultimate truth that all perceived separations are mere illusions. Here, this truth resonates deeply, as I witness the interplay of forms and the seamless harmony amid apparent chaos. The ego’s insistence on individuality fades as a witness consciousness arises, truly observing the expanse with the understanding that I am not separate from this beauty but an integral part of it.

## A Sloka for the Path

"अथ चित्ते न विचिकित्सा, अवश्यं च ज्ञानप्रबंधनः।\n संशयात्मा विनश्यति।"

_"atha citte na vicikit-sā, avaśyaṃ ca jñāna-prabandhaṇaḥ।\n saṃśayātmā vinaśyati।"_

“When doubt ceases in a knowing mind, their prior uncertainty vanishes.”

This verse from the Bhagavad Gita resonates deeply during this part of the journey. As pilgrims, our doubts and uncertainties often weigh heavily upon us. Yet, amidst the high altitude, the clarity of the setting and this sacred venture dissipate these clouds of doubt. The path teaches persistence, as does the clarity and focus that dedication to such an endeavor can bring. In the face of the mountain’s permanence and the challenge it poses, inner silence emerges, nurturing a mind that can grasp wisdom without question.

## Practical Travel Notes

My journey from Darchen to Dalmala Pass carries with it memories robust with the vividness of sensory immersion. The cold morning air felt strikingly thin, giving each breath a sharp edge, a reminder of the altitude's demanding presence. As I layered up, the wool and synthetic fibers blended into a comfortable cocoon, shielding me against the wind that whipped down the mountain.

Riding a horse on this rugged path was both an alien and comforting experience. The rhythmic motion rocked gently, making it easier to lose oneself in contemplation, while also keeping me constantly aware of each precarious step on the loose stones underfoot. Other pilgrims, their breaths visible in the air, shared quiet exchanges that blended with the soft whistling of the wind—creating a harmony of human interaction and nature's whisper.

Meals were simple yet grounding, consisting of warm broths and stale bread that filled the stomach but more importantly, they fueled the soul. There's a camaraderie formed when breaking bread amidst such an expanse, where the simplest of sustenance feels sacred.

## Closing Reflection

Reaching the Dalmala Pass after a grueling day of ascent feels like touching a place beyond the physical—a sanctuary of sorts, where the internal and external merge seamlessly. The effort exerted physically parallels the spiritual exertion required to continue a path of self-exploration. The swirling mists around the peaks carry with them not just cold, but a revitalizing clarity that touches the soul.

This journey, undertaken with silent steps of surrender and acceptance, leaves an indelible mark. As I stand upon the pass, the collective ache of exertion blends into a singular sensation—a profound gratitude for the immense gifts that such a formidable pilgrimage imparts. With each step downward, a humble promise is etched within: to carry this expanded awareness into the fabric of everyday life, to be reminded always of the simplicity and profundity found on the paths of Mount Kailash.
