Vishishtadvaita means "non-dualism of the qualified" — or more precisely, the oneness of Brahman as qualified by its inseparable modes of souls and matter. Ramanuja stands as both a philosopher and a saint, writing with equal force in Sanskrit commentary and in the devotional warmth of the Sri Vaishnava tradition he inherited from the Tamil Alvars.

His philosophy was forged in conscious opposition to Advaita. Where Shankara saw the world as mithya (apparent), Ramanuja saw the dismissal of the world's reality as philosophically untenable and spiritually damaging — it leaves no room for genuine love between devotee and God.

## The Body-Soul Relationship

Ramanuja's signature contribution is the *sharira-shariri* (body-soul) metaphor. Just as a soul is real and the body is also real, yet the body has no independent existence apart from being sustained and governed by the soul — similarly, individual souls and the material world are real, yet they subsist entirely within Brahman, sustained and governed by him.

This preserves both:
- **The reality of the world** (against mayavada, the view that the world is mere illusion)
- **The absolute supremacy of Brahman** (there is nothing outside him)

## Three Ultimate Realities

1. **Ishvara** — the Supreme Being, Narayana, the inner ruler
2. **Chit** — sentient souls, eternally real and distinct, yet dependent on Ishvara
3. **Achit** — insentient matter, the substrate of embodied experience

All three are real. All three are also one, in that chit and achit are the body of Ishvara.

## The Path of Bhakti and Prapatti

Ramanuja gave philosophical grounding to the intense devotional poetry of the Alvars. The path is *bhakti yoga* — sustained, loving meditation on the personal God — culminating in God's grace reaching down to the devotee. The surrender (*prapatti* or *sharanagati*) is not passive resignation but a complete turning of oneself toward Vishnu as the only refuge.

This tradition lives powerfully in the Sri Vaishnava temples of South India — in Srirangam, Tirupati, and Kanchipuram.
